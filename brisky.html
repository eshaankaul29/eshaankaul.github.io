Brisky (Consumer-End)

Brisky is a recommendation platform that connects people with local experts for personalized recommendations. It also provides real-time information about what is happening at pubs around you.


Key metrics include: 30,000+ users across iOS and Android. 5,000+ MAUs- 8,000+ recommendations. 28-Day retention of 30%.


Rethinking Brisky

We defined our primary user story as “I want to find something fun to do this weekend”. To meet that need, we reshaped our event discovery experience across our ecosystem to bring more life, levity, and fun to the experience.


To become a trusted destination we needed to transform our data to be more city-centric, integrate curated editorial content. For this effort, my role was to redesign and systematize the visual language across the Android and iOS app as well as lead the product vision for the social feeds.


Through various channels of understanding user journeys, we were able to identify a significant growth opportunity in the social segment, which was the most vital features that I launched. In the Social Segment, users could interact with their peer groups and come to a consensus about a place or an event.


Creating focused experiences

To create a fun browse experience we needed to build an onboarding that would be both delightful to complete and useful in shaping our user's discovery experience. Restaurant profiles and event listing were both experiences we wanted to reinforce Restaurant recognition and build trust around content.

Brisky Business

Brisky Business helps brick and mortar businesses(restaurants) automate their customer interaction. Automating critical tasks like customer re-engagement, feedback collection, etc. helps these businesses in providing a superior customer experience.


In Brisky Business, I laid the foundation of the product whose primary users were check-in representatives of restaurants and their managers. We have observed that the restaurant industry has always had a great desire to adopt new technologies and make data-driven decisions. However, they are not willing to put the effort of going through charts, deriving correlations and splicing data sets to get the insights. What they are looking for is someone to tell them what actions they should take.


Key Metrics Include: 60 paying clients with ARR of $40,000. NPS of 76. Achieved net churn of 20%. DAU/MAU ratio of 80%, reflecting the high usage of the product.
